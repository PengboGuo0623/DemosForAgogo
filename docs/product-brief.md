# Math Rescue Product Brief

## Summary

`Math Rescue` is a lightweight H5/Web math education game demo for children
ages 5-8. The working Chinese title is `数字救援队`.

The demo should show a short, friendly, low-frustration math interaction where
children answer simple addition and subtraction questions to help rescue small
friends, stars, or number characters.

This is a showcase demo, not a complete commercial product.

## Audience

- Primary users: children ages 5-8.
- Secondary viewers: parents, educators, product stakeholders, and app teams
  evaluating whether the experience direction is promising.

Children in this age range may have uneven reading ability, short attention
spans, and different math confidence levels. The experience should rely on
clear visuals, large touch targets, and supportive feedback.

## Product Goals

- Demonstrate a complete 60-90 second math play loop.
- Make simple arithmetic feel like helping, rescuing, and celebrating.
- Keep the session short enough for a quick product review.
- Show a mobile portrait H5 experience that can later fit inside an app host.
- Establish a clean technical foundation using Vite, TypeScript, and Phaser.

## Current Platform Scope

- Current target: H5/Web demo that runs in a browser.
- Future possible target: app-hosted WebView.
- Current phase: no App SDK integration.
- No native bridge, account system, backend, analytics, ads, payments, or
  persistent storage are required.

## Experience Principles

The demo may reference high-level patterns common in children's education
products:

- bright color
- rounded shape language
- cartoon-friendly presentation
- short play sessions
- low frustration
- strong positive feedback
- minimal text
- clear visual guidance

These are experience principles, not asset references.

## Originality Rules

Do not copy Lingokids or any other product's specific:

- characters or mascots
- UI screens or layouts
- illustrations
- sound effects
- music
- text or catchphrases
- brand assets
- recognizable visual identity

All Math Rescue characters, scenes, UI, and feedback language must be original
or clearly generic.

## MVP Outcome

The first playable MVP should let a child:

1. Start the game from a friendly start screen.
2. Answer 8 simple math questions.
3. Choose from 3 answer options per question.
4. See a rescue progress moment after correct answers.
5. Receive gentle encouragement after wrong answers.
6. Finish with a positive result screen.

## Non-Goals

- No full curriculum progression.
- No adaptive learning engine.
- No user profiles.
- No monetization.
- No multiplayer.
- No production content management.
- No real App SDK integration.
